The income gap refers to the unequal distribution of income between different groups of people, where some individuals or groups earn significantly more than others, leading to disparities in living standards and access to resources."

Income Gap

The income gap has been increasing in the United States since the 1970s. According to a 2018 report by the Economic Policy Institute, the top 1% of earners saw their wages increase by 157% between 1979 and 2007, while the wages of the bottom 90% of earners only increased by 22%.
